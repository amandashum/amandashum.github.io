Pothole Detection with Relative Depth Estimation

Sept – Dec 2025 • Computer Vision, Depth Estimation

Developed a computer-vision system that automatically detects potholes and estimates their depth using only a single RGB image by combining YOLOv8 with MiDaS monocular depth estimation.

Integrated YOLOv8 for pothole localization and MiDaS for dense depth maps, then implemented region extraction around bounding boxes to compare pothole depth against the surrounding road surface. Built depth-normalization, noise-filtering, and relative-depth scoring modules to quantify pothole severity without requiring stereo cameras or LiDAR. Evaluated the pipeline on real road images and achieved stable depth differentiation across varying lighting, texture, and perspective.
